# D2C Performance Marketing Core Principles

This document distills 42 proven D2C frameworks into a concise reference used by the Creative Ad Audit template. Cite framework names inline when making recommendations so the user can trace the rationale.

---

## 1. Creative Conversion Framework — Five Storytelling Structures

Pick ONE structure per ad. Do NOT mix emotional triggers in a single creative.

### PAS — Problem · Agitation · Solution
- **Problem**: name a specific friction the customer feels today (1 sentence).
- **Agitation**: amplify the cost of doing nothing (time, money, embarrassment).
- **Solution**: introduce the product as the direct relief. End with a clear CTA.
- **Use when**: customer awareness is high and the pain is easily recognized (e.g., back pain, slow WiFi).

### BAB — Before · After · Bridge
- **Before**: describe the prospect's current state factually, without judgment.
- **After**: paint the future state after using the product (vivid, sensory).
- **Bridge**: the product is the literal bridge between the two states.
- **Use when**: the transformation is the strongest selling point (weight loss, skincare, productivity).

### Social Proof Loop
- Lead with a quantitative proof point ("37,482 customers", "4.8★ avg").
- Insert a customer quote that targets the primary objection.
- Close with an implicit "join them" CTA.
- **Use when**: the brand has credible volume and the category is trust-sensitive (supplements, finance, SaaS).

### Myth Breaker (Contrarian)
- Open with a widely held belief: "Everyone says X..."
- Flip it: "...but the data/experience shows Y."
- Reveal the mechanism (why the myth persists) and the product's solution.
- **Use when**: competing with an established category narrative (e.g., fasting, cold showers, meal prep).

### Future State Formula
- Project the customer forward 30/60/90 days: "Imagine waking up and..."
- Tie specific product features to each step of the projected state.
- End with "and it starts today with [CTA]".
- **Use when**: the product is a habit/system (subscriptions, fitness, education).

## 2. Hook Testing Taxonomy

A hook is the first 3 seconds (video) or first headline (search/display). Classify every hook into ONE of seven trigger families; mixing triggers dilutes performance.

| Trigger | When it works | Example pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Curiosity | Informational gap | "Why [expert] stopped recommending [category]" |
| Contrarian | Established wisdom to flip | "Stop [common behavior]. Do this instead." |
| Emotional | Strong felt pain | "If [pain] is ruining your [life area]..." |
| Logical | Data-oriented buyer | "3 numbers that explain why [problem]" |
| Outcome | Clear result promise | "How I [specific result] in [timeframe]" |
| Social Proof | Category trust needed | "[Big number] of [peers] switched to..." |
| Urgency | Genuine scarcity only | "[Date]: [offer] ends. Not extending." |

**Rule**: never fake urgency. Ethical urgency ties to a real deadline (season end, batch cap, price rise date).

## 3. Offer Engineering

A creative cannot outrun a weak offer. Audit offers across three dimensions:

### Value Stack (additive, not discount)
- Bundle core product + 2–4 relevant bonuses, each with a stated retail value.
- Total stated value should be ≥ 3x the price.
- Bonuses must reduce friction (shipping, setup, guarantee) or amplify outcome (templates, coaching, access).

### Risk Reversal
- Money-back guarantee (60–90 days minimum for consumables).
- "Keep the bonuses" on refund.
- Performance-based language ("If you don't see X, we'll refund AND...").

### Ethical Urgency
- Real deadline (campaign end, cohort start, batch production).
- Capacity cap ("first 500 customers get the founder bonus").
- Price anchor ("price rises to $X on [specific date]").

**Anti-patterns**: countdown timers that reset, "only 3 left" when stock is unlimited, "50% off" as the default price.

## 4. Ad Fatigue Detection & Recovery

### Signals (in order of severity)
1. CTR drops ≥30% week-over-week while CPM stays flat.
2. Frequency > 4 (Meta) or > 7 avg impressions/user (Google Display).
3. CVR drops while CTR stays flat → offer/landing page fatigue, not creative.
4. Negative sentiment comments rising → creative is irritating.

### Diagnosis matrix
| Symptom | Likely cause | First move |
|---|---|---|
| High CTR, low CVR | Landing page or offer mismatch | Align LP headline to ad hook |
| Low CTR, high CVR | Audience saturated, message stale | Refresh hook (same offer) |
| Low CTR, low CVR | Full creative exhaustion | New concept, not iteration |
| Rising CPM | Auction pressure, not fatigue | Check bid strategy, not creative |

### Refresh cadence
- Cold audiences: new primary creative every 14–21 days.
- Retargeting: new creative every 7–10 days.
- Always test ONE variable at a time (hook OR offer OR visual).

## 5. Profitability KPI Glossary

These metrics live below the surface of ROAS. Audit all of them before scaling.

### Core formulas
- **MER** (Marketing Efficiency Ratio) = Total Revenue ÷ Total Ad Spend. Safe scaling threshold: **≥ 4.0** for most D2C categories.
- **Cost Efficiency** = (Revenue − Ad Spend) ÷ Ad Spend. Positive = profitable; target ≥ 1.0 after CM2.
- **Break-even ROAS** = 1 ÷ Gross Margin. Below this, every scaled dollar destroys value.
- **LTV:CAC** = Lifetime Value ÷ Customer Acquisition Cost. Health: **3:1 or higher**. Below 2:1 = unsustainable.

### Contribution Margin layers
- **CM1** = Revenue − COGS (product cost, shipping, payment fees). Must be ≥ 40% to support paid media.
- **CM2** = CM1 − Marketing spend. This is the number that funds scaling.
- **CM3** = CM2 − Fulfillment/ops overhead. This is take-home margin before fixed costs.

### ROAS vs MER
ROAS is platform-attributed and inflated by branded search + retargeting. MER is truth. Whenever ROAS > MER × 1.5, attribution is double-counting; trust MER for budget decisions.

## 6. Seven Deadly Scaling Mistakes

Screen every scaling decision against these. Any ONE of them voids the case for raising budgets.

1. **Scaling on ROAS alone** — ignoring MER and CM2. Sells good-looking reports, destroys cash.
2. **Tracking blind** — no server-side tracking, no Consent Mode V2, no deduplication. Scaling amplifies noise.
3. **Premature channel expansion** — launching TikTok + YouTube + Meta simultaneously before any channel has stable CAC.
4. **Margin blindness** — assuming last quarter's CM2 holds under promotion-heavy scaling.
5. **Creative burnout denial** — pushing budget into fatigued creatives, CPMs explode.
6. **Cash flow gap** — ad spend compounds immediately, revenue lands 14–60 days later. Scale within payment cycle capacity.
7. **Ignoring retention** — new customer CAC rises as the serviceable audience shrinks. No retention = no LTV = no scaling ceiling.

## 7. Google Ads Audit Kit — 30-minute diagnostic

Run through these in order. Each finding is either a Profit Leak (fix today) or a Growth Opportunity (add to quarterly plan).

1. **Campaign structure**: Branded vs non-branded isolated? PMax not cannibalizing Search?
2. **Negative keywords**: Are low-intent terms (job, salary, free, DIY) excluded?
3. **Match types**: Broad match used? If so, is Smart Bidding the strategy?
4. **Bid strategies**: Target CPA/ROAS aligned to CM2-based break-even, not to vanity targets?
5. **Ad Strength**: Every Search ad group has ≥ 1 RSA at GOOD or EXCELLENT strength?
6. **Ad extensions**: Sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets, images all populated?
7. **Conversion tracking**: Enhanced Conversions on? Consent Mode V2 live? Server-side via GTM?
8. **Audience signals**: Customer Match lists feeding into PMax and Search audiences?
9. **Placement exclusions** (Display/PMax): Mobile app categories excluded? Known waste domains blocked?
10. **Asset reporting**: PMax asset group performance labels reviewed monthly? LOW-labeled assets rotated out?

## 8. Precision Scaling Checklist — Green/Yellow/Red Gates

Before raising account-level budget > 15% in a week, all GREEN gates must be clear:

- GREEN: MER ≥ 4 for 21 consecutive days, CM2 ≥ target, conversion tracking validated this week.
- YELLOW (proceed with caution): MER between 3–4, one tracking anomaly, one creative in fatigue.
- RED (do not scale): MER < 3, CM2 turned negative in trailing 14d, conversion tracking broken, new payment processor not settled.

Rule: **never scale budget and creative in the same week**. Isolate variables.

## 9. Attribution Clarity Principles

- Treat platform ROAS as directional, not absolute.
- Run weekly MER vs ROAS diff report. If drift > 20%, investigate de-dup.
- Branded search + retargeting get discounted attribution weights in internal dashboards (50% of platform-reported).
- Incrementality tests (geo holdout or platform pause) once per quarter on the top-spend channel.

## 10. Ad Rewrite Rules (Section 6 of the Audit)

When producing 3–5 optimized variations:

1. Assign each variation ONE framework from Section 1 above. Never mix.
2. Single variable discipline: all variations keep the same product, same offer, same URL. Vary only the creative structure.
3. Each variation must include: Framework name | Headline(s) | Description(s) | CTA | Rationale | Expected lift (be honest — "testable hypothesis, not guaranteed").
4. Rationale cites the specific fatigue signal or audit finding the rewrite addresses.
5. Prefer ethical urgency and real social proof numbers over fake scarcity or vague superlatives.
